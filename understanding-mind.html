Understanding Mind

Mind Processes Information

Our body is controlled by forces that seem to be beyond our understanding. We can move our arm when we want to but sometimes it moves without our conscious command. We can conjure movement of our body because we intend the movements. We move through the physical environment using our senses to monitor the surroundings and adjust our steps accordingly. We avoid obstacles and manouvre through the environment with relative ease, coordinating the information entering our eyes with the muscles of our body.

With sufficient information, it should be possible to build up an understanding of the relationship between these forces and the control mechanisms that have evolved to maintain the survival of the individual.

The individual, a physical body with a mind somehow attached to it. We are told that we have a mind of our own, however, there is no explanation as to what 'mind' actually is, where it is and how different it may be from the next person's mind.

Mind has been removed from academic discussion, starting a couple of hundred years ago and ramping up in the past fifty years. In Victorian times, Psychology was the study of Mind and Soul, why has the concept that is 'mind' been removed from everyday language? Today, students are taught that intelligence is within the brain structure. Somehow, (allegedly), certain molecules can manifest intelligence, consciousness and decision making processes, only when they are part of a physical structure called a brain. For the most, those molecules just behave like inanimate, mindless and lifeless physical structures with no possibility of directing movement or holding memories.

Mindism is the term I use to define a doctrine that enables understanding of most things in the universe. It states that there are three distinct realms of existence that together form the world we perceive and know. The mind of each individual exists and experiences each realm, they are not separate but distinct and Mind experiences all three 'at the same time'.

Convention states that there are three levels of mind, conscious, sub conscious and unconscious. Using this convention I use the term conscious mind to refer to what we experience in the 'here and now', conscious mind monitors 'the moment and makes 'real time' decisions accordingly. Conscious mind (usually) controls our speech centre and, for as long as we are awake, informs 'us' of how we are, how we feel and considers what we should do next. Conscious mind monitors our awareness and perception of our immediate reality, both internal and external in real time. It receives information from our senses, 'makes sense ' of it and makes decisions accordingly.

When the physical body dies, conscious mind can no longer function because there is no sensory input to process. When the body is alive, sensory input; smell, taste, vision, touch etc. deliver information to the mind where it is processed accordingly.

Mind processes information, in other articles I have written I explain that 'the information' is 'the flowing point' of a waveform frequency and that it is the interaction of these frequencies (mathematically represented as 'If-Then' statements) that manifest the concept we call Intelligence.
